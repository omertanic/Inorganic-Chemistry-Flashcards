⚡ Memory tricks before the table

  • Nylon 6,6 = two 6-carbon monomers (hexamethylenediamine + adipic acid). "6,6 = two hexes shaking hands"
  • Nylon 6 = one monomer (caprolactam), ring opens. "6 alone, breaks its own ring"
  • Buna-S = Butadiene + Styrene. Buna-N = Butadiene + Acrylonitrile (Nitrile). S=Styrene, N=Nitrile.
  • Bakelite = Phenol + Formaldehyde. "Bake it in a mould" → thermosetting, can't remould
  • Dacron/Terylene = ethylene glycol + terephthalic acid → polyester (ester linkage)
  • Teflon = tetrafluoroethylene. All F's → nothing sticks → non-stick pan
  • Vulcanisation = sulphur crosslinks at 373–415K. Makes rubber harder + less sticky
  • Natural rubber = cis-1,4-polyisoprene. Gutta percha = trans-1,4-polyisoprene (hard, non-elastic)

Key Concepts — Frequently Tested

Vulcanisation of Rubber

  • Heating raw rubber with sulphur at 373–415 K
  • S forms crosslinks at double bond sites
  • Tyre rubber: 5% sulphur
  • Fixes: softness at high T, brittleness at low T, water absorption
  • Makes rubber harder, elastic, durable

Addition vs Condensation

  • Addition: unsaturated monomers, no byproduct, chain growth
  • Condensation: bifunctional monomers, loses small molecule (H₂O, HCl, alcohol)
  • Nylon, Dacron, Bakelite = condensation
  • All rubbers, PVC, Teflon = addition

Thermoplastic vs Thermosetting

  • Thermoplastic: softens on heating, can remould — linear/slightly branched
  • Thermosetting: hardens permanently, cannot remould — cross-linked
  • Bakelite, Melamine = thermosetting
  • Nylon, PVC, Polythene = thermoplastic

LDP vs HDP Polythene

  • LDP: high pressure (1000–2000 atm), 350–570K, free radical, branched
  • HDP: low pressure (6–7 atm), 333–343K, Ziegler-Natta, linear
  • HDP = harder, more tough, closer packing
  • LDP = flexible, squeeze bottles

Molecular Forces → Properties

  • Elastomers: weak van der Waals, coiled chains
  • Fibres: strong H-bonding (nylon) or dipole interactions → high tensile strength
  • Plastics: intermediate forces
  • Nylon H-bonds → crystalline, high tensile

Novolac → Bakelite

  • Phenol + HCHO (acid cat.) → Novolac (linear, used in paints)
  • Novolac + more HCHO → Bakelite (cross-linked)
  • Bakelite = infusible solid, thermosetting
  • Melamine = harder than Bakelite
